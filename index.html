Admiral (Ret) John S. McCain Sr.

The Grandfather of Senator John S. McCain and Father of Admiral (Ret) John S. McCain Jr.

  • 1884 - Born on January 17th in Council Bluffs, Iowa
  • 1906 - McCain graduates from the United States Naval Academy and is commissioned as an ensign in the United States Navy.
  • 1910 - McCain marries Katherine Vaulx, with whom he would have three children, including John S. McCain Jr., who would later become a four-star admiral and the father of Senator John McCain.
  • 1915 - McCain receives his first command, the destroyer USS Ammen.
  • 1917-1918 - McCain serves as the commander of the destroyer USS Warrington during World War I.
  • 1927 - McCain graduates from the Naval War College in Newport, Rhode Island, and later serves as an instructor there.
  • 1933-1936 - McCain serves as the commanding officer of the aircraft carrier USS Ranger, one of the first carriers in the US Navy.
  • 1941-1942 - McCain serves as the chief of staff to the Commander-in-Chief of the Pacific Fleet, Admiral Husband Kimmel, and is present during the attack on Pearl Harbor on December 7, 1941.
  • 1942-1943 - McCain serves as the commander of the aircraft carrier task force that conducted the first offensive carrier raids against Japanese-held islands in the Pacific during World War II.
  • 1944-1945 - McCain serves as the commander of Task Force 38, a fast carrier task force that played a key role in the battles of the Philippine Sea and Leyte Gulf, among others.
  • 1945 - McCain is promoted to the rank of Vice Admiral and becomes the deputy chief of naval operations (logistics) at the Navy Department in Washington, D.C.
  • 1948 - McCain retires from the Navy after over 40 years of service, having received numerous awards and honors for his distinguished career.
  • 1961 - John S. McCain Sr. dies on September 6th at the age of 77 and is buried at Arlington National Cemetery in Virginia.
